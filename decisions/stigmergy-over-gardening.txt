# Stigmergy over gardening

> http://localhost:3000/kern/decisions/stigmergy-over-gardening/

## Decision

Forgetting is driven by a decaying `heat` scalar deposited by use — reads deposit, an hourly collector evicts what goes cold — with no retention policies, quotas, or manual curation.

## Why

- Three knobs total: deposit magnitude, evaporation rate, threshold floor. Everything else emergent.
- Policy knobs multiply — every administered rule ("but keep facts") is another interacting special case.
- Retention becomes a consequence of use, not a policy someone maintains.

Shipped constants:

- Read deposits `1.0` heat, off the query hot path. It is the only deposit.
- Decay is lazy, from last-deposit age; half-life is preset-owned (`relaxed` 30 days by default); a deposit decays the stale value first, never resurrecting old magnitude.
- Hourly GC demotes entities that are cold (aged heat below `0.01`), stale (untouched 7 days), and not durable — spilled to the cold tier, not deleted.
- Net: one read survives 6.64 half-lives — ~**199 days** on `relaxed`, ~46 on `medium`, ~20 on `tight`; repeat reads never decay past half peak.

## Rejected

| Alternative | Why it lost |
| --- | --- |
| TTLs | delete the fact you needed on day 31 |
| LRU / quotas | evict by an ordering (insertion, size, age) unrelated to usefulness |
| Manual gardening | doesn't scale past one attentive operator; silently stops happening |
| Per-edge pheromone | storage cost for a weak signal; traversal counts feed scoring, not retention |
| Multiple pheromone types | one scalar suffices; entity kinds already provide orthogonal typing |
| Cross-peer heat gossip | a peer's usage is not evidence of yours; an injection surface |

## Trade-off accepted

- Heat is retention-only; ranking keeps its own two-term freshness boost with a 24-hour half-life — two freshness signals, by design not unification.
- The half-life errs heavily toward retention (6.64 half-lives per read, not one, because the cold gate is `0.01` and not half peak).
- Convergence metrics never built — the access-distribution claim is design intent, not measurement.
- The pulse used to deposit too, and a recurring deposit of *any* size settles far above the `0.01` gate: everything within its 5-level reach was permanently exempt from collection, used or not. Removed — retention is a consequence of use only (`tests/depth_bias.rs`).

> **One administered exception.**
Active `Fact` and `Document` entities are immune to collection (locally — peers
cannot pin storage by labelling gossip `kind=Fact`). Supersession revokes the
immunity. See [Heat & compaction](http://localhost:3000/kern/concepts/heat-and-compaction.txt).


## Where you see it

- Unused memories quietly demote to the cold tier once heat clears the floor — months on `relaxed`, weeks on `tight`; anything you recall stays hot.
