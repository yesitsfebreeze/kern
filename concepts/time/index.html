Concepts

Time & contradiction

Every thought carries two clocks: when a statement was true in the world, and when kern found out. A contradicted claim is superseded — invalidated, kept as history — never deleted; query what was true, or what kern believed, at any instant.

Why revision is first-class

"Deadline is March", then "moved to April": a vector store holds both — near-identical embeddings, opposite meanings — and returns whichever ranks higher. Deleting the old one loses "what did we think in January?", the audit trail, and the evidence it moved at all.

Two clocks

ClockFieldsMeaning
World time[valid_from, valid_to)When the statement was true out there. Half-open — revisions tile without overlap. Distillation can extract an explicit valid_from.
Transaction timecreated_at / invalidated_atWhen kern's own store changed.

They come apart routinely: learn Friday about Monday's reversal; backfill six months in an afternoon; "what did the system believe on the 3rd?" vs "what was true on the 3rd?".

A thought with no temporal information is always considered valid — the conservative default; the alternative would silently hide everything written before the temporal machinery existed.

Supersede

Two paths, same mutation: explicit re-ingest under the same external id, or a contradiction verdict from the tick. Either way:

  1. New thought inserted and indexed normally.
  2. Old one's status → Superseded, pointing at its successor.
  3. Transaction time stamped now; world-time window closes when the new claim became true — clocks set independently.
  4. Old id leaves the vector indexes but stays in its kern — no longer retrieved, history recoverable.

A Supersedes edge links new to old.

Contradiction detection

Never at query time, never synchronously at ingest. Ingest records a Rephrase edge; the background tick asks the LLM one constrained question — UPDATE, CONTRADICT, or RELATED.

  • Recall stays LLM-free — decision made before any query arrives.
  • Fails open to coexistence — hedged, empty, or unparseable = RELATED. A missed contradiction leaves two coexisting claims (recoverable); a false supersede hides a true claim (not).
  • Kind gates the verdict — only a same-kind near-duplicate may supersede; a distilled Claim never invalidates a user-asserted Fact.

Querying the past

Two independent query options; they compose into the revision chain as it stood at a past instant:

  • as_of <timestamp> — pure world-time filter: the single revision whose validity window covered that instant. Exact over the cold tier too.
  • include_history — normal query, then walks the supersede chain backward from each hit; ancestors flagged history:true (recovered from cold if needed). Widens results without loosening filters.

as_of ≠ valid_at. valid_at checks an explicit expiry date stamped on a thought; as_of checks the supersede-maintained validity window. A claim can be unexpired yet superseded, and vice versa.

What time does not do

Bi-temporality governs correctness (which revision is current), not retention. A superseded thought must still go cold before GC touches it — supersession only makes it eligible (revokes a Fact's eviction immunity). A queried superseded claim stays hot; an untouched uncontradicted one goes cold. See Heat & compaction.
