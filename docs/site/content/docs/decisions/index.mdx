title: "Design decisions"
---

Every non-obvious mechanism in kern exists because an alternative was
considered and rejected. This section is the rationale of record: one page per
decision, each stating the constraint that forced a choice, the alternatives
that failed, the reasoning that picked the winner, and — honestly — which parts
of the original design have shipped and which have not.

These pages are ported from the research notes in
[`docs/kern/`](https://github.com/yesitsfebreeze/kern/blob/master/docs/kern/README.md)
and re-verified against the current source tree. Where a note assumed something
the code no longer does, the page says so. Code references are current
`src/…:line` locations, not the note's historical ones.

## Self-organization

- **[Stigmergy over gardening](./stigmergy-over-gardening.mdx)** — why the
  graph maintains itself through use-marks (heat, decay, eviction, clustering)
  instead of manual curation or scheduled cleanup jobs.
- **[PageRank for authority](./pagerank-authority.mdx)** — why retrieval
  weights results by graph centrality rather than raw similarity alone.
- **[Bayesian confidence](./bayesian-confidence.mdx)** — why belief in a claim
  is a Beta distribution updated by independent observations, not a counter or
  a flag.
- **[Edit convergence](./edit-convergence.mdx)** — the Wikipedia-NPOV-style
  model for how contradicting observations converge on durable, neutral
  thoughts.

## Federation

- **[CRDTs over consensus](./crdts-over-consensus.mdx)** — why replicas merge
  through commutative joins instead of electing a leader or taking locks.
- **[Knowledge, not gradients](./knowledge-not-gradients.mdx)** — why kern
  gossips typed claims between peers instead of doing federated learning.

## Retrieval & indexing

- **[DiskANN-style spill](./diskann-spill.mdx)** — why the on-disk ANN index
  exists, and why it is an opt-in spill rather than the default backend.

For how each mechanism actually behaves today, the [Concepts](../concepts/why-kern.mdx)
pages are the reference; these pages explain why it behaves that way.
