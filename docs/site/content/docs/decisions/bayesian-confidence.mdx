title: "Bayesian confidence"
---

**A thought's confidence is a Beta distribution — pseudo-counts
`conf_alpha`/`conf_beta` updated by weighted observations — not a scalar that
the latest writer overwrites.** This shipped in full on every entity
(`src/base/types.rs:262`): ingest seeds the tuple from the declared confidence
exactly as the design note proposed, corroborating observations move it, and
the derived mean is what ranking multiplies by. What did **not** ship is the
explicit contradiction surface — there is no `Contradicts` edge kind, no
`stance` parameter on ingest, no observer-reputation weighting, and no evidence
decay.

## Context

kern's thesis is that in a substrate shared by many agents, **convergence
across independent observers is the truth signal**. One agent asserting X is
weak evidence; ten agents from different contexts asserting X should approach
certainty; one dissenter in that population should be absorbed, not dominate.

A scalar confidence cannot represent any of that. It has no variance and no
sample count, so "one observer, conf 0.9" and "twenty observers, mean 0.9" are
indistinguishable — yet they warrant completely different treatment when
observer twenty-one disagrees. And every update rule available to a scalar is
bad: overwrite is last-writer-wins, max lets a single overconfident source pin
the value forever, and averaging forgets how much evidence the average stands
on.

## Alternatives considered

- **Keep the scalar** (status quo ante). Fails as above: no way to distinguish
  corroboration from assertion, and a lone `conf=1.0` write fixes truth.
- **Integer support/oppose counters.** Capture sample size but not observer
  confidence — a tentative guess and a firm assertion each count 1 — and yield
  no principled uncertainty measure.
- **Full belief networks** (propagating conditional dependencies between
  claims). The canonical machinery, and wildly over-scoped: kern's claims are
  not wired into a dependency DAG, and the inference cost would sit on the
  ingest path.
- **Beta-distributed belief** — the conjugate prior for a Bernoulli "is this
  proposition true". Two floats, closed-form updates, mean and variance for
  free. Adopted.

## Why the Beta tuple wins, with the shipped numbers

The update rules are three one-liners on `Entity` (`src/base/types.rs:394`):

```
observe_support(w):    conf_alpha += clamp(w, 0, 1)
observe_contradict(w): conf_beta  += clamp(w, 0, 1)
mean = α / (α + β)        variance = αβ / ((α+β)²(α+β+1))
```

The clamp is the outvoting property: no single observation, however shrill, is
worth more than one full vote.

Seeding maps the ingest `conf` onto a uniform prior
(`beta_params_from_confidence`, `src/ingest/place.rs:16`):
`(α, β) = (1 + conf, 1 + (1 − conf))`. The prior biases the opening posterior
toward the observer's stated confidence without committing to it.

Worked through with real path values — distilled claims enter at `conf = 0.6`
([Acceptance & routing](../concepts/acceptance.mdx)):

| Event | α | β | mean | variance |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| First ingest at `conf 0.6` | 1.6 | 1.4 | 0.533 | 0.062 |
| A second, independent observation (dedup hit, `+0.6`) | 2.2 | 1.4 | 0.611 | 0.052 |
| Eight more concordant observations | 7.0 | 1.4 | 0.833 | 0.015 |
| One full-strength dissent (`observe_contradict(1.0)`) | 7.0 | 2.4 | 0.745 | 0.018 |

Two observations move a coin-flip prior to a modest lean; ten make it a tight
belief; one dissenter against ten is visible — an 0.09 drop — but nowhere near
decisive. That asymmetry between "moves the needle" and "pins the needle" is
the entire point, and no scalar update rule produces it.

The tuple then flows into ranking through one function: `refresh_score`
(`src/base/types.rs:390`) writes the posterior mean into `Entity.score`, and
`apply_boosts` multiplies each result's retrieval score by it
(`src/retrieval/score.rs:82`). Corroborated claims outrank asserted ones with
no extra machinery.

## Who actually observes

Three shipped writers, one of which the note never anticipated:

- **Ingest dedup.** A near-duplicate at or above the 0.95 threshold is treated
  as evidence, not discarded: `merge_duplicate` calls
  `observe_support(new_score)` on the existing entity
  (`src/base/accept.rs:57`, reached via `src/ingest/dedup.rs:23`). Text and
  vector are never overwritten — the id is the content hash — so
  re-observation reinforces belief without mutating identity.
- **GNN propagation.** After each learned re-embedding pass, the cosine
  alignment between an entity's content vector and its new structural vector
  is read as an observation: alignment `≥ 0.5` supports at weight `w`,
  misalignment contradicts at `1 − w` (`src/tick/gnn_propagate.rs:154`). An
  entity whose graph neighbourhood keeps agreeing with its content gains
  confidence; one the structure pulls away from loses it. This is currently
  the **only** caller of `observe_contradict` in the tree.
- **Trust boundaries.** Under federation the tuple is deliberately
  replica-local: `conf_alpha`/`conf_beta` are never imported from peers
  (`src/base/merge.rs:93`, pinned by poisoning tests), and remote-kern
  entities have theirs zeroed. A peer can gossip a claim; it cannot gossip
  *belief in* a claim. Ten peers asserting X only converges a replica that
  ingests X ten times through its own gate.

<Callout type="info" title="Contradiction resolves by supersession, not by β">
The note proposed routing semantic contradictions into `β`. What shipped is
sharper: a same-kind near-duplicate whose text conflicts goes to an LLM
classifier on the background tick, and an unambiguous verdict **supersedes**
the old claim outright rather than down-weighting it
(`src/tick/tasks.rs:114`). Beta confidence answers "how corroborated is this
claim"; supersession answers "which revision is current". Keeping them
separate avoids the degenerate state where a superseded claim lingers at
mid-confidence. See [Time & contradiction](../concepts/time.mdx).
</Callout>

## What shipped vs. what the note assumed

The note (`docs/kern/bayesian-belief.md`) predates the current layout; its
core proposal — the tuple, the seeding formula, fractional-weight conjugate
updates, backward-compatible derived scalar — shipped essentially verbatim.
The deltas:

- **No `ReasonKind::Contradicts`.** The edge vocabulary
  (`src/base/types.rs:66`) has `Rephrase` and `Supersedes`; explicit
  contradiction edges were not added, and contradiction is handled by the
  supersession path above.
- **No `stance` parameter on ingest.** Every ingest observation is support;
  `observe_contradict` is reachable only from GNN alignment. An agent cannot
  yet register disagreement short of asserting a conflicting claim and letting
  the classifier rule.
- **No observer-reputation weighting.** The note planned to scale `w` by a
  per-producer reputation composed with [PageRank
  authority](./pagerank-authority.mdx); neither half was built.
- **No evidence decay.** `α` and `β` only grow. Stale consensus therefore
  takes proportionally many new observations to unseat, rather than decaying
  toward the prior; the tick-based `γ` damping remains an open design.
- **Reason edges do not carry belief.** They kept a scalar `score` with
  last-writer-wins CRDT metadata (`src/base/types.rs:418`), not a tuple — the
  note's open question, resolved toward "no" by default.
