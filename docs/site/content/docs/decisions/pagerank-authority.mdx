title: "PageRank for authority"
---

**kern ranks with a damped, personalised PageRank over the reason graph, fused
by rank into retrieval's seed set — not with a global authority score, and not
(yet) with the federated trust machinery the original design centred on.** The
retrieval half shipped and is on by default: `pagerank`
(`src/retrieval/pagerank.rs:28`) runs at damping `0.85`, up to `25` iterations,
teleporting at the query's own seeds, and its top-`100` enter RRF seed fusion
(`src/retrieval/answer.rs:121`). The federation half — per-peer authority,
TrustRank seeding, the layered Sybil defences — was **not built**.

## Context

Vector similarity answers one question: *what looks like the query*. It cannot
answer *what does the neighbourhood of things that look like the query keep
pointing at*. A well-cited hub two hops from every seed — the entity a dozen
reasons converge on — scores nothing on cosine if its own text happens not to
match. The graph knows which entities are structurally load-bearing; before
this decision, ranking never asked it.

The design note (`docs/kern/pagerank-authority.md`) framed the same gap one
level up: in a federation, a peer cited by thousands of other peers was treated
identically to a brand-new peer nobody had ever referenced. One mechanism —
eigenvector-style centrality — plausibly serves both scopes, which is why the
note evaluated them together.

## Alternatives considered

| Method | What it measures | Why not |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Degree centrality | Raw inbound citation count | Trivially gameable — N fake citers buy N units of rank; no transitivity, so a citation from a hub counts the same as one from a leaf. |
| Eigenvector centrality | Fixed point of adjacency × score | Undamped: diverges on dangling nodes, no convergence guarantee on disconnected components, no recovery from adversarial sinks. |
| HITS | Separate hub and authority scores | A second eigenvector to maintain, duplicating what damped PageRank already captures; its bipartite framing fits query/doc graphs, not a general reason graph. |
| SALSA | Stochastic HITS | Same objection, more machinery. |
| TrustRank | PageRank personalised at known-good seeds | The right Sybil defence for federation — retained in the design for that scope, unbuilt because that scope is unbuilt. |

PageRank with damping is the smallest member of the family that handles
dangling nodes, cycles, and disconnected components while staying a single
vector with a convergence guarantee.

## Why the shipped variant wins

Three properties of the implementation carry the decision.

**Personalisation makes it query-aware.** Global PageRank has a failure mode
the note itself flagged: a globally popular entity rides to the top of every
query, relevant or not. The shipped teleport vector is built from the query's
dense and lexical seed hits, weighted by their scores (`teleport_vector`,
`src/retrieval/pagerank.rs:5`) — so the random walk restarts *at the query*,
and rank mass concentrates in the query's own graph neighbourhood. The
query-independent importance scan is deliberately excluded from the teleport
set (`src/retrieval/answer.rs:122`) for exactly this reason. One subtlety is
load-bearing: dangling mass is redistributed along the teleport vector, not
uniformly (`src/retrieval/pagerank.rs:58`) — uniform redistribution would leak
personalisation back into a global prior on every iteration.

**Damping `d = 0.85` bounds both divergence and collusion.** At `d = 1` the
walk never restarts and dangling nodes absorb everything; at `d → 0` the graph
is ignored. `0.85` gives an expected walk length of `1/(1−d) ≈ 6.7` hops —
long enough to see real structure, short enough that a long chain of
mutually-citing entities cannot launder rank down its length. Self-loops are
neutral by construction (pinned by test:
`self_loops_do_not_inflate_score`, `src/retrieval/pagerank.rs:150`). Iteration
stops early once the rank vector moves less than `1e-9` L1
(`src/retrieval/pagerank.rs:49`), so the `25`-iteration ceiling
(`src/config/retrieval.rs:131`) is a bound, not a cost.

**Rank fusion sidesteps score calibration.** The note weighed two integration
points — an additive `authority_weight` term in boost scoring, or a fourth
list in reciprocal-rank fusion — and preferred RRF as the cleaner design. RRF
is what shipped: the PageRank top-k joins the dense, lexical, and importance
lists at weight `rrf_global_weight = 0.5` against `1.0` for dense and lexical
(`src/retrieval/answer.rs:135`), and fusion consumes only ranks, never the raw
PageRank masses. There is no `authority_weight` knob in the config, and no
score-magnitude interaction to mis-calibrate.

A worked example, straight from the test suite
(`personalization_biases_toward_seed_and_conserves_mass`,
`src/retrieval/pagerank.rs:249`): a graph with two symmetric components,
`B → A` and `Y → X`. Unseeded, `A` and `X` tie exactly. Seed the walk at `Y` —
as a query whose dense hits land in that component would — and `X` outranks
both its unseeded self and `A`, while total rank mass still sums to 1. That is
the whole mechanism in miniature: structure decides *within* the
neighbourhood the query selects, and cannot decide *instead of* it.

Where PageRank sits in the full pipeline — after seeding, before expansion —
is covered in [The retrieval pipeline](../concepts/retrieval.mdx).

<Callout type="warn" title="The unit mechanism works; the end-to-end effect is measured at zero">
Everything above is pinned by unit tests over `pagerank()` in isolation, and
those hold. What does not follow is that the authority signal reaches a
delivered result. `e2e/test_invariants.py` drives the whole CLI query path —
the same `cfg.retrieval`, so PageRank and expansion are both on — ingests A and
B, adds an A→B reason edge, and finds that the edge moves one of 8 pairs by one
rank. An edge is exactly the input this mechanism consumes, so that bounds how
much of the delivered ranking graph structure currently decides. Item 86 in
[`ROADMAP.md`](https://github.com/yesitsfebreeze/kern/blob/master/docs/oracle/ROADMAP.md)
carries the diagnosis.
</Callout>

## What shipped vs. what the note assumed

The note is a federation-era design (it references the pre-1.0 `crates/`
layout throughout) and most of its surface area is unbuilt:

- **No DB-level authority.** The `AuthorityTable` — one rank scalar per peer,
  fed by cross-DB reference edges — does not exist. No per-peer authority
  signal reaches retrieval or routing.
- **No TrustRank seeding.** The shipped teleport vector is per-query seed
  hits, not a curated set of known-good peers. The Sybil-resistance property
  TrustRank was chosen for is therefore not provided.
- **No incremental (ACL push) computation.** The note specified
  Andersen–Chung–Lang push updates to amortise cost on a live graph. Shipped
  is a full power iteration per query over the entity adjacency — affordable
  at thought scale with early exit, and nothing is persisted between queries.
- **None of the layered Sybil defences** — edge-weight caps, pulse-coupled
  edge validation, temporal slashing of frequently-superseded producers —
  are in effect. Two were written and never connected: a per-peer
  `RateClipper` (in the since-deleted `gossip/sybil.rs`, 175 lines) whose
  `set_clipper()` was never called from anywhere, and
  `trimmed_mean_merge_hits` (`gossip/merge.rs`, 241 lines), documented in its
  own comment as "a Sybil-resistant alternative" for fusing per-peer hit
  lists, which had no caller either. Both were deleted in `dc02a18` as
  verified-unreachable code.
  Removing them changed no runtime behaviour, because neither had ever run —
  they are unbuilt defences that happen to have a reference implementation in
  git history, not defences that were lost. Federation trust today rests on
  the transport and merge rules described in
  [Federation](../concepts/federation.mdx), not on rank.
- **Thought-level rank feeding split heuristics** (the note's phase 2) was
  not built; clustering and splitting run on cosine cohesion alone.

<Callout type="warn" title="Read the note's security sections as unimplemented design">
The note's §5 describes defences for an authority system kern does not
currently run. Since no rank crosses a machine boundary — each replica
computes PageRank over its own local graph, per query — the Sybil surface the
note defends against does not exist yet either. If DB-level authority is ever
built, that section becomes a prerequisite, not an option.
</Callout>
